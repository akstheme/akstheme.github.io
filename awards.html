Recognition

Awards & Honors

A collection of recognitions, fellowships, and distinctions earned across research, academia, and open-source contributions.

2025 Fellowship

Google PhD Fellowship in Machine Learning

Google — Mountain View, CA

Awarded to outstanding PhD students making exceptional contributions to AI and ML research. One of 70 recipients globally across 16 research areas. Provides full tuition support, stipend, and Google mentorship for 2 years.

2024 Best Paper

Best Paper Award — CVPR 2024

IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition

Awarded for the paper "Cross-Modal Grounding with Hierarchical Attention for Visual Question Answering." Recognised among 2,700+ accepted papers for exceptional novelty, technical rigour, and empirical results.

2022 Fellowship

Prime Minister Research Fellowship (PMRF)

Government of India — Ministry of Education

India's most prestigious PhD fellowship, awarded to top 3% of PhD applicants at IITs and IISc. Provides ₹80,000/month stipend plus research grant of ₹2 lakh/year over 4 years for AI research with societal impact.

🎓
2021 Academic

Best Master's Thesis Award

IIT Delhi — Department of Computer Science & Engineering

Awarded for the thesis "Efficient Self-Supervised Representation Learning for Small-Scale Medical Datasets." Recognized as the best thesis in the M.Tech batch of 2021 for academic excellence and practical impact.

2023 Research Grant

Microsoft Research Grant for AI in Healthcare

Microsoft Research India

Competitive research grant (₹15 lakh) awarded to support the MedSight clinical AI project. Includes access to Azure compute credits, collaboration with Microsoft Research India scientists, and potential for product integration.

2022 Honorable Mention

ACM SIGCHI Honorable Mention

ACM Special Interest Group on Computer-Human Interaction

Received honorable mention for the paper on accessible AI interfaces for visually impaired radiologists, highlighting the intersection of AI and human-computer interaction at CHI 2022. Top 5% of accepted papers.

German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD)

Awarded a 3-month research fellowship at the Technical University of Munich (TUM) in collaboration with Prof. Matthias Nießner's 3D AI Lab. Conducted research on neural radiance fields and 3D scene understanding.
